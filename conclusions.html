CONCLUSIONS

Loss of Smell
  • More than 60% of people diagnosed with coronavirus experienced loss of the sense of smell
  • It is often one of the first symptoms experienced by patients
Loss of Taste
  • Loss of sense of smell and taste indicates a 10 times higher chance of coronavirus than any other infection
  • Compared to other symptoms, people with loss of sense of smell and taste are 3 times more likely to have coronavirus
Fever and Tiredness
  • Almost 90% of coronavirus victims present fever as a symptom
  • However, milder cases show much lower percentages of patients with fever
Shortness of Breath
  • Almost 90% of coronavirus victims present fever as a symptom
  • However, milder cases show much lower percentages of patients with fever
Headache
  • Around 15% of coronavirus cases report headaches as a symptom
  • Headaches are most probably asasociated with fever, and are often dependent on it
Chills
  • As seen in studies, 10% of those infected suffer from repeated or intense bouts of chills
  • This symptom was added to the CDC's list of symptoms of the disease and is highly indicative of a coronavirus infection
Coughing
  • Approximately 65% of infected people experience dry coughs
  • Less frequently, around 30% of patients display coughing with excess sputum
Bluish Face and Lips
  • This is indicative of a lack of oxygen and may be a warning sign of pneumonia
  • It is an emergency symptom of coronavirus, and a professional should immediately be contacted
Foot Lesions
  • Often called 'Covid toes' these are most often seen in infected children
  • It has a good prognosis and is usually the first or only symptom of infection
Lack of Mental Awareness
  • 11% to 44% of hospitalized coronavirus patients reported exhaustion and fatigue as symptoms
  • Large studies from China and France show that 36% of patients experience neurological symptoms
Rashes
  • Studies show that around 20% of coronavirus cases show dermatological symptoms
  • These are most commonly red itchy rashes on the torso and limbs
Loss of Appetite
  • Approximately 40% of cases involve a loss of appetite as a digestive symptom
  • This can occur without evidence of any respiratory symptoms
Dizziness
  • Around 10% of patients experience dizziness, making up a large proportion of neurological symptoms
  • Nervous system issues are more common among older patients with secondary illnesses
Chest Pain
  • Elderly patients or those with chronic health conditions are most likely to feel chest tightness and pain
  • Only around 2% of hospitalized patients state chest pain as a symptom, but many may develop this at a later stage
Nausea and Vomiting
  • 10% of patients appear to experience nausea when infected and most do not require ICU admission
  • Vomiting presents as a symptom in less than 5% of cases, indicating that it is not strongly connected to a coronavirus infection
